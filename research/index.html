    Research

    My research interest lies in the intersection between bio-inspired robotics design, multi-sensor fusion and machine learning (especially morphological computing) and their applications in the real world, e.g., robot perception, object recognition & localisation, and multimodal sensing fusion.

    Please email me if you are interested in my research and I’m open to any potential collaboration.

    Finished Projects

    1. Tapered Whisker Based Neuromorphic Computing System for Mobile Robot Environment Perception and Navigation
      • Affiliation: Morph Lab, Imperial College London with Prof.Thrishantha Nanayakkara, Prof. Peter Childs, Dr. Sadati, S. H , S. Perer, and Prof.Helmut Hauser
      • Objective: The overarching objective of this project is to develop a tapered whisker reservoir computing system for the first time where the mobile robot exploits the complex nonlinear dynamics of physical whisker bodies as a computational resource for terrain identification, texture estimation, and navigation.
      • Funding body: This work was supported in part by the EU Horizon 2020 “ Natural Intelligence for Robotic Monitoring of Habitats，EU Horizon 2020” under Grant Agreement 101016970, UK Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) project MOTION – Morphological Computation of Perception and Action, under Grant EP/N03211X/2, EP/N029003/1, UK Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) project RoboPatient: Robot-Assisted earning of Constrained Haptic Information Gain .
